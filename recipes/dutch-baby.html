Dutch Baby

About the Dish

The Dutch baby is a pancake baked in the oven that originated in the US in the early 1900s. I like mine sweet, with some powdered sugar and strawberries.

The recipe source: once upon a chef.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1 tbsp of maple syrup
  • 1/4 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 tsp of vanilla extract
  • 3 tb of unsalted butter
  • powdered sugar (optional)
  • strawberries (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F & set an oven rack.
  2. Insert a 10-inch cast iron skillet into the oven and heat for at least 5 minutes.
  3. Combine eggs, flour, milk, maple syrup, salt, and the vanilla extract in a blender. Blend until smooth (~30 seconds). This is the batter.
  4. Put butter on preheated skillet. Close the oven and allow the butter to melt (~2 minutes).
  5. Remove hot skillet from oven.
  6. Pour the batter into the skillet and place skillet back into the oven.
  7. Bake for ~20 minutes, until puffed and golden.
  8. Remove skillet from oven. Dust with powdered sugar and top with strawberries.
